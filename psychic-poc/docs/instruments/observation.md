# Instrument: Observation (Appearance & Tells)

> Builds on [`../reading.md`](../reading.md). An **evidence instrument** — not a divination method. It
> supplies facts about the person, not "book" meanings.

## What it is

**What the client wears and how they present.** Clothing, grooming, jewelry, posture, wear-and-tear —
the player reads these as **tells** about the client's life: their means, their occupation, their
mood, a recent loss, a wedding ring's presence or absence. This is straight **cold reading**, and it's
deeply on-theme: the "not a psychic" gets his real information the way real fake psychics do —
by paying attention.

## How it's used in the deduction

Observation supplies **facts about the person** that the divination instruments get read *against*.
The same tarot card means one thing for someone in mourning black and another for someone in a
work uniform. Tells help the player pick the reading that actually fits, and they surface as
color-coded keywords the dialog can reference.

## Open questions

- **How tells are surfaced** — clickable details on the client portrait, a highlighted list, pure
  observation? (See [`../art-direction.md`](../art-direction.md).)
- **How tells map to facts** — one-to-one, or do they require interpretation (and can they mislead)?
- **Reliability** — can appearances be deliberately deceptive, adding deduction depth?
- **Volume** — how many tells per client, and how much they matter vs. the divination instruments.
