# Instrument: Tarot Spread — the centerpiece

> Builds on [`../reading.md`](../reading.md) (the client puzzle). Tarot is the primary divination
> instrument; every client has one.

## What it is

A **predetermined spread**: cards laid into positions, **hard-set per client** — not drawn at random.
Each **position** carries its own significance (e.g. past / present / future, or situation / obstacle
/ outcome), and each card carries a **fixed meaning** from the tarot "book." The spread is the
backbone of the reading; the other instruments corroborate or complicate it.

## The book — card meanings

Each card has a canonical meaning the game treats as correct (*The Tower* = sudden upheaval, *The
Hermit* = withdrawal/introspection, …). Simplified meaning tokens are fine to keep the book legible.
The player learns these from the in-world **tarot reference book** (see
[`../art-direction.md`](../art-direction.md)).

## How it's used in the deduction

The player reads the laid-out spread against the book **and the client's evidence** — the same card
means something different for a widow than for a newlywed, and the correct read is the one that coheres
with the sign (astrology), the hand (palmistry), and the tells (observation, identity). Getting that
synthesis right is "solving" the tarot half of the puzzle; dialog that reflects it aligns.

## Client affinity

Even as the centerpiece, tarot isn't universal: a client may believe in it strongly or **reject** it.
Using a method a client rejects is a **noticed error** that drains suggestibility (see
[`../reading.md`](../reading.md#method-affinity--what-clients-believe-in)).

- **How tightly the spread cross-references** the other instruments — can tarot alone solve a client,
