# Instrument: Identity (ID / Wallet)

> Builds on [`../reading.md`](../reading.md). An **evidence instrument** — the interactable object the
> player explores at intake. Not a divination method.

## What it is

During the initial intake, the client provides an **interactable object** — an ID, a wallet, or
something like it — that the player can **pick up and explore**. This is the game's Papers-Please
document-inspection beat: poke around and pull **hard facts** out of it.

Likely contents (varies per client): **name, birth date, address, occupation, photos, receipts,
cards, keepsakes.** The exact object can differ from client to client (part of the object's
character).

## How it's used in the deduction

Identity is the **anchor evidence** the other instruments key off:

- The **birth date** feeds the [star chart](./astrology.md).
- The **occupation, address, and contents** corroborate (or contradict) the [observation](./observation.md)
  tells and steer how the [tarot](./tarot.md) spread should be read.

Because it's explorable, it rewards curiosity — the player who actually digs finds the facts that make
the reading click. Facts pulled from it surface as color-coded keywords the dialog can reference.

## Open questions

- **What the object is** — always an ID, always a wallet, or does it vary per client for flavor?
- **How exploration works** — free interaction with the object, or a set of inspectable spots? (See
  [`../art-direction.md`](../art-direction.md).)
- **How much it reveals**, and whether facts can be **contradictory, incomplete, or withheld** to
  deepen the deduction (a Papers-Please-style discrepancy hunt).
- **Consent/fiction** — how the game justifies the player rummaging through a client's wallet
  (handed over willingly? snooped?). Tone matters.
