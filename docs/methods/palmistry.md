# Method: Palm Reading (Palmistry)

> **Status: STUB.** Placeholder capturing the method's skeleton against the shared framework in
> [`../divination-methods.md`](../divination-methods.md). To be detailed like
> [`tarot.md`](./tarot.md) when this method is scheduled.

Palmistry is the **"close observation of a fixed subject"** method — the counterpoint to tarot's
sequential draw. The subject doesn't change randomly the way a deck does; the challenge is careful
*reading* of what's there.

## Contract (to be filled in)

- **Subject.** The client's hand — the major lines (heart, head, life, fate), plus their length,
  depth, breaks, and intersections.
- **The book — fixed meanings.** What each line and feature canonically signifies (e.g., what a long
  vs. short vs. broken line means). The standard the Score is judged against.
- **Step reveal + interpretation choices.** How features are surfaced in sequence (the player
  identifies/traces lines) and the interpretive choice made at each — the "two valid directions"
  principle applies here too.
- **Aggregation into advice.** How the per-feature reads combine into the final outcome that feeds the
  client's story.
- **Correctness check.** How faithful line-reading is scored for accuracy.

## Open questions specific to palmistry

- **Where does the randomness live?** Tarot randomizes the draw; a hand is comparatively fixed. Is the
  hand itself randomized/generated per client, and/or is randomness in which features are legible or
  ambiguous? This must satisfy the non-scripted / non-look-up-able requirement.
- **What's the pseudo-puzzle?** Identifying/measuring lines correctly before interpreting — the exact
  interaction is TBD.
- **Relationship to tarot's model.** How much of tarot's framework transfers directly vs. needs a
  palmistry-specific twist.
