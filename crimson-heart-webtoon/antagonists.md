# Crimson Heart — Antagonists (The Enemy)

**Phase:** SEED. A pool of archetypes, held deliberately light.

The big-bads are **loosely based on Mughal Ganjifa**, the eight-suit Indian card
game. That gives **eight enemy archetypes**, split into a **strong** group and a
**weak** group.

**How to use these:** we're **not** going to characterize these eight deeply or
give each a full story. They're an **archetype pool** — when a role needs filling,
pull whichever of the eight fits the bill. And eight enemies building toward a final
confrontation is a solid arc in itself, so they can simply stand as the **enemy
leaders Crim and crew have to defeat**, one after another.

## The Strong — might, leadership, power

The bigger bad guys, representing the enemy's leadership and raw power.

- **Silver**
- **Crown**
- **Sword**
- **Servant**

## The Weak — bureaucracy, the grunt work

The lesser ranks, representing the enemy's bureaucracy, functionaries, and legwork.

- **Gold**
- **Harp**
- **Goods**
- **Decree**

---

### Reference: the Ganjifa suits

For art/naming reference only (not canon detail) — the eight suits of a Mughal
Ganjifa deck and the English names used above:

| English (used here) | Traditional suit |
|--------------------|------------------|
| Silver | *Zar-i-safed* — white/silver coin |
| Gold | *Zar-i-surkh* — red/gold coin |
| Sword | *Shamsher* — swords |
| Crown | *Taj* — crown |
| Servant | *Ghulam* — servants/retainers |
| Harp | *Chang* — harp |
| Goods | *Qumash* — cloth/merchandise |
| Decree | *Barat* — documents/decree |

> Note: our **strong/weak** split is a creative choice for this story, not a claim
> about how the ranks work in the historical game.
