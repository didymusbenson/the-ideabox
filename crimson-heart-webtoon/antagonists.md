# Crimson Heart — Antagonists (The Enemy)

**Phase:** SEED. A pool of archetypes, held deliberately light.

The enemy is built on the backbone of **Tujeon** (투전), the traditional Korean
card game. This is a deliberate upgrade from an earlier Ganjifa draft: tujeon has a
command hierarchy *baked into its rules*, which is exactly the shape an antagonist
faction wants.

## Why tujeon — the structure is the point

A tujeon deck is organized into **eight suits**, and each suit is nine numeral
cards **led by a single General (장, *jang*)**. That gives a native two-tier enemy:

- **The eight Generals** — the big-bad **leaders**, one per suit. The boss fights.
- **The numeral ranks beneath each General** — that suit's rank-and-file: the
  functionaries, the bureaucracy, the grunt work.

So the "leadership vs. grunt work" split that used to be a horizontal strong/weak
grouping is now **vertical** — each General sits atop their own suit of lesser
enemies. Eight Generals building toward a final confrontation is a solid arc on its
own, and every General comes with a themed army pre-attached.

**How to use these:** still an **archetype pool**, still held light — we're not
deeply characterizing all eight up front. When a role needs filling, pull the
General (or a numeral grunt) that fits.

## The first foe — the North Star (Star suit)

Crim's **first General** is the **North Star**, head of the **Star (별)** suit. This
is a deliberate hook back to the [original chapter 1 concept](./chapter-1-concept.md):
the threat arrives as a **shooting star that slams into a man's chest and possesses
him**. That falling-star possession is the Star suit's calling card — the North
Star's forces raining down and hijacking civilians — which makes it the natural
opening skirmish and the first General Crim has to put down.

## The rank and file

Beneath each General sit their suit's **nine numeral ranks** — the everyday
enemies. These are the bureaucracy and legwork of the enemy: possessed civilians,
minor manifestations, the disposable threats Crim clears in seconds. They're where
the "monster fights are short and to the point" tone lives; the Generals are the
ones that actually cost her something.
