# Crimson Heart — Premise & Pitch

**Phase:** SEED. This is the author's premise in prose, lightly organized. Not a spec.

## Logline

A magical-girl team saved the world and grew up. Years later the threats return —
and the retired, endgame-powered, grief-scarred Crimson Heart has to suit back up,
not to relive her glory days, but to keep the fight from swallowing her daughter
and the quiet life she fought to build.

## The setup

A magical girl team defeated the big bad and saved the world. The threats stopped
coming, so the team grew up and moved on with their lives in whatever ways they
could.

Bunny didn't move on cleanly. Her **tuxedo-mask analog was killed in the final
battle**, sacrificing himself for her, and it broke something in her. She
eventually **moved to the US to get away** from it all, found a guy, settled down,
and had a couple of kids — until **one day the bad guys come back** and it all
starts up again.

The **stupid talking cat** that roped her into the life the first time is now
trying to **recruit her daughter** — she's just about the right age and has the
genes / magic / whatever to join the fight. Bunny's answer is "fuck that," and she
**powers back up herself** instead.

So now we've got a moody, trauma-filled, **endgame-powered** magical girl fighting
off these threats — and she is *piiiiiiiissed*.

## What the story is actually about

The threats are not the point. The story centers on **Crim (Bunny) navigating the
new threats while trying to hold onto her hard-won peace and work through the PTSD
of her former magical girl life.** Its real subject matter is:

- **Family dynamic** — protecting her daughter from getting pulled in, and getting
  her **husband to understand that all those bedtime stories are true.**
- **Being a magical *woman*** rather than a magical girl — confronting what the
  role means from the other side of adulthood, motherhood, and loss.
- **Friendship** — reconnecting with her former teammates, who all have full lives
  of their own now.
- **Processing trauma and healing** — the throughline the whole thing is built on.

## Tone: the fights are short on purpose

Bunny is **fully powered** — endgame strength, right out of the gate. **Monster
fights are incredibly short and to the point; the enemies are no match for her
ultimate attack.** The action is deliberately anticlimactic so the weight lands on
the aftermath: the domestic life, the relationships, the grief. The tension isn't
"can she win the fight," it's "what does winning keep costing her."

## The team — a suit of four

The team is themed on the **card suits**. Season one is a **core four**, one per suit:

| Suit | Magical name | Civilian calling |
|------|-------------|------------------|
| ♥ Hearts | **Crimson Heart** (Bunny) | Stay-at-home mother of two (the protagonist) |
| ♠ Spades | **Raven Spade** | Space researcher *or* single mother — binding TBD |
| ♦ Diamonds | **Amber Diamond** | Nightclub dancer, using her magical-girl alias as her stage name |
| ♣ Clubs | **Onyx Club** | Space researcher *or* single mother — binding TBD |

The warm/red suits carry warm names (**Crimson** Heart, **Amber** Diamond) and the
black suits carry dark names (**Raven** Spade, **Onyx** Club).

Bunny's former teammates have all moved on and built civilian lives. They're
**noticing the patterns too** — that the threats are creeping back. Bunny
**initially tries to keep everyone out of it** because she can handle the threats
herself, and she's generally right about that.

**Callings.** With the **clergy/priest role removed** (folded into the single
mother), the three teammate callings are: **space researcher**, **single mother**,
and **nightclub dancer**. Amber Diamond is the dancer (she carries "Amber" forward
from the old *Amber Embrace*). Which of **Raven Spade** and **Onyx Club** is the
space researcher versus the single mother is still open.

**Possible expansion (post–season one).** The author may add **Swords** and **Cups**
as **secondary suits** later — new characters, *not* part of the season-one core four.
