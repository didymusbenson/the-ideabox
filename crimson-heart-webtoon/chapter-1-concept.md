# Crimson Heart — Original Chapter 1 Concept

> Transcribed verbatim from the author's notebook. This is raw source material —
> the "original chapter 1 concept," preserved as-is. It predates the fuller
> premise and pitch (still to come) and should be treated as a historical draft,
> not the current spec.

---

Bunny Westlake is your average every-day stay at home mother of 2, but once upon a time she was Crimson Heart, the magical girl who protected the world from the forces of evil known as the Demon Gang. After defeating them once and for all, there was nothing to do but settle down and start a family. Some of her friends from those days also moved on with their lives.

Scarlet Soul – Clergy – Alias:
Sapphire Mind – Space Researcher – Alias:
Emerald Fist – Single Mother – Alias:
Amber Embrace – Pop Singer – Alias:

Although they keep in touch, Bunny hasn't seen her old friends in years — so when a new enemy appears in the middle of a mommy-daughter shopping trip, Crimson Heart is forced to transform once more.

---

[Someone is narrating the story of Crimson's last mission. We start with the final attack, a] FINAL CRISIS: EXPLOSIVE HEART DEVASTATION! We come out of the action to see it's Bunny telling the story to her daughters at bedtime. They ask if Crimson Heart is real, and Bunny tells them "of course," with a wink. They say goodnights and Bunny crashes onto the couch with her husband, propping her feet up in his lap. "Kids asleep?" "Yep. Now it's just you and me." They hang out for a bit before a fade-to-black. The next day Bunny & husband are making breakfast together while the older kid rushes to get ready for school. Hubby goes to work while Bunny drives them. On the way, the younger girl spots a shooting star

(the star follows us — the star slams into a man's chest & he screams in pain) Kid 1 is dropped off and Bunny & Kid 2 head off to the store to do shopping for the week. Bunny laments how expensive eggs are getting when the star-struck man enters the store. He starts making a scene, heading straight for the bakery aisle to tear into bags of sugar. Bunny is concerned and keeps Kid 2 safe as security hauls the man out of the store. They check out. "Mommy, what was that man doing?" "Probably drugs. We just need to be careful, and if anything dangerous like that happens we need to just get away, ok?"

"JUST RUN AWAY? IS THAT WHAT I TAUGHT YOU TO DO?" says a cat on a ledge. Bunny's face contorts in fear as she sees the cat. "SHIT!" She rushes Kid 2 to the car and peels away. "Did that cat just talk?" "Mommy we're not supposed to say shit!"

They get home and Bunny rushes Kid 2 inside with the groceries. The cat is by their front door trying to say "Bunny!" but she slams the door and hastily throws the food into the fridge & cupboards. She turns on the TV to distract Kid 2 and picks up the phone, clearly having an anxiety attack. We see flashes of memories. "This is what happened back then, shooting stars, talking cats, it's all happening again." "Hubby! Are you at lunch?" Husband replies yes. "Is everything alright" — more flashbacks of Crimson Heart holding a dead person in her arms — "Can you please come home? I'm having a... a hard time." "Uh, sure. Absolutely, I'll let them know I'll be with the rest of the day." "Please hurry!"

Hubby calls back a minute later as the cat starts scratching at the window. "Hey babe, I'm coming as fast as I can, but there's something going on in Main Street. I'm locked in traffic and — OH SHIT" — crashes and explosions are heard after the phone — "I'm good, I'm safe. Uh, I'll be there as soon as I can."

Bunny looks at the phone, the cat, her child. "Stay here, don't go anywhere! Ms. Neighbor will be here in just a minute." "Ok Mommy!" Bunny runs and pounds on the next door neighbor's house. "Ms. Neighbor!" She answers and Bunny explains it's an emergency, she needs someone to watch her kid. All the while the cat is following. "Thank you," she shouts as she pulls out of the driveway to head back to town. The cat is on the passenger seat. "Finally got you alone, then, didn't I?" The cat explains. "Shut up, Kitty, last time you came into my life you ruined EVERYTHING. I'm done. I have a life now. Kids! A husband. NO MONSTERS!" She's crying. "It's PERFECT!"

"Perfect though it may be, you're still the chosen here. That man you saw at the store? That was no addict. It was the enemy."

(We see them pull up to the scene on Main Street where the man from earlier is screaming and pounding cars.)

Bunny sees her husband in another car nearby. "We need you again, Bunny. The world needs you." Bunny fumbles with her purse, digging through spare tampons, makeup, other purse junk until she finds the hidden compartment with her transformation stick.

"I thought I was done with this. Those days were behind me. But I'll be damned if I let that thing lay a finger on my family."

We get a power shot of Bunny stepped out of the car (low angle but not upskirt) then a signature pose. "SHINE HEART POWER! SPARKLE!"

[Two to 3 page transformation sequence as Bunny's plain persona fades away into a magical girl transformation, complete with the hair, the bows, and the miniskirt.]

"HEY JACKASS!" She shouts at the enemy, who turns and bellows. "I don't know where you've come from, but you should know — this world is protected. I am CRIMSON HEART. I'll stop you with the POWER OF LOVE!" She points and uses an attack that causes the enemy to stagger back. The shooting star crashes out of its back and shatters. A normal man collapses on the ground in tattered clothes.

Bunny arrives home and dismisses the neighbor. She crashes on the couch and looks at her 4yo, who's still watching TV. It's a news report showing the fight in downtown. Husband walks in holding Kitty.

"Hey, I came home as soon as I could, you'd never believe what just happened! — Uh, that doesn't matter right now, are you ok?" He sets Kitty aside and sits on the couch, holding Bunny close as she holds back tears. "Yeah, it's ok. We can talk later. I —"

"SHIT." We cut away to the car. "What?" "I left the milk in the back seat."

End of chapter — has the milk in back of car.
