# Crimson Heart — Chapter Pacing

**Phase:** SEED → early structure. Author's page-by-page breakdown of the opening.

This paces the material from the [original chapter 1 concept](./chapter-1-concept.md)
across **two** published chapters (the notebook's single "chapter 1" was always
closer to a two-chapter arc). Convention in the opening leans on **one panel per
page** for tension beats (see Ch. 1, pp. 9–11). Excerpts are quoted from the
transcript; **page 4 introduces new revised dialogue** not in the original notebook.

## Notes & connections

- **Symmetry:** both chapters land at exactly **19 pages**.
- **The enemy on Main Street** is the star-struck man from the store — a possessed
  civilian, i.e. the [Star suit's falling-star possession](./antagonists.md) that
  marks the **North Star** as Crim's first General. Ch. 2 p. 15 is that first
  cleansing (star shatters, civilian freed).
- **The dead love interest** now appears as early as **Ch. 1 p. 4** (a panel on the
  title page, tied to the "happy ending" question) and again in the **p. 18**
  flashback — still unnamed. See the open thread in [`open-questions.md`](./open-questions.md).
- The p. 4 "happy ending" exchange is the emotional thesis in miniature: Bunny tells
  her kids the story ended happily while a panel shows the cost it actually carried.
