# Inspiration reference — Sky Factory 4 advancements

**What this is:** a captured reference of Sky Factory 4's advancement (achievement) system, kept as
*inspiration* for TFT's own progression and advancement design. It is **not** a spec to copy. Per the
pitch's "original everything" pillar, TFT ships its **own** advancement names, resources, and art — the
value here is the *structure and comedic register*, not the literal content.

**Provenance / caveats:**
- Source: the Sky Factory 4 Fandom wiki (`skyfactory-4.fandom.com/wiki/Advancements`), which is blocked
  by this environment's egress policy and could not be fetched directly. The content below was
  **pasted in by hand**, so treat it as a partial, human-copied snapshot rather than an authoritative dump.
- **Partial:** only three pages were captured — the starter page, *Age of Enhancement*, and *Age of
  Farming* (which is truncated at the end). SF4 has further ages beyond these that aren't here.
- Several entries in the later ages had **no description** in the paste (marked "— *description not
  captured*"); listed counts don't always match the number of rows captured, noted where relevant.

## How SF4 organizes advancements (the part worth stealing)

- **Optional, not a quest gate.** The book is framed as "ideas on what to do next," never a forced path.
- **Pages = "ages,"** each themed around a specialism (starter/bootstrapping, machine enhancement,
  farming, …). Each age is a self-contained board of goals.
- **A prerequisite graph, not a line.** Early advancements list **parents** (sometimes *multiple*
  parents), forming a soft tech-tree rather than a linear checklist.
- **Every age ends in a capstone — "You Win!"** — awarded for completing every advancement on the page,
  worth **1 prestige point**. Prestige points buy permanent upgrades carried into all future runs.
- **Pun-driven naming.** Nearly every advancement is a pop-culture or wordplay gag.

## What TFT should borrow (and what to leave)

**Borrow the structure:**

1. **Sapling-per-resource is the whole spine.** The overwhelming majority of the starter age is
   "craft an **X** Sapling" — dirt → clay, gravel, bone → coal, redstone, lapis → tin, lead, iron →
   silver, gold, diamond. Each resource *literally becomes a tree.* That's TFT's "every grind becomes a
   tree" already proven as a progression skeleton, and it hands us a ready resource-tier ladder
   (basic → building → metal → precious).
2. **New trees come from combining a resource with an existing tree.** The pattern "pour **Molten
   [metal]** over a **[specific] Sapling** → **[metal] Sapling**" is a clean crafting-graph idea: you
   unlock a resource-tree by *applying a processed resource back onto a base plant.* Great fit for TFT —
   twerk a base plant for output, process it, feed it back to unlock the next plant.
3. **Ages as prestige units.** Each themed page ends in a **"You Win!"** capstone worth **1 prestige
   point** → permanent upgrades. This maps 1:1 onto TFT's "replant the world" prestige. Structure TFT
   progression into **named ages**, each capped by a prestige-granting capstone.
4. **Soft prerequisite tree, not a line.** Multi-parent advancements gate *suggestions* without forcing
   order. TFT's unlocks can branch the same way — visible next steps, not a corridor.
5. **A capstone that requires clearing the whole board** creates a natural "one more advancement" pull
   right before prestige.
6. **Verbs beyond "craft."** SF4 rewards *obtain, cook, collect rain, sleep, breed, level up, combine,
   fill a chest.* TFT should vary its advancement triggers too — plot counts, dirt crafted, twerk
   streaks, first automation, prestige counts — not just "unlocked plant X."
7. **Note the built-in tap-to-hasten precedent:** *Sticky Keys* rewards speeding tree growth by
   spamming LSHIFT near a sapling. That's essentially the twerk, and it's worth being aware TFT is
   formalizing a mechanic SF4 already gestured at.

**Leave behind:**

- **The literal names and IP.** Every title here is a specific pun tied to Minecraft/mod content
  (Lead Zeppelin, Clay Aiken, Sixteen Tons…). TFT copies the **comedic register** — dense, groan-worthy
  wordplay — with its **own** original gags. Do not reuse these.
- **Mod-specific machinery** (Tinkers', Mekanism, NuclearCraft, Smeltery, etc.) — those are the *feel*
  of "enhancement/automation ages," not mechanics to port.
- **Sheer count.** SF4's board is huge because it's a sprawling modpack. TFT's vertical slice needs one
  age's worth of advancements at most; scale later.
