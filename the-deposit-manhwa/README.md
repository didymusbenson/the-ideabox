# 전세 게임 (JEONSE GAME) — Series Bible

Working English title: **The Deposit**

A landlord loses his tenants' entire jeonse deposits in a bad real estate gamble. To repay them before the law — and the tenants themselves — catch up to him, he's pulled into an underground tournament where the games are rigged, the buy-in is the very money he owes, and his opponents are the people he betrayed.

**Genre:** Psychological thriller / survival game / social drama
**Format:** Webtoon-native, vertical scroll, full color. ~120–150 episodes across 3 seasons, arcs of 12–16 chapters.
**Tone anchor:** *Squid Game* meets *Itaewon Class*, with the specific, currently-real anxiety of Korea's jeonse fraud (전세사기) crisis instead of an invented dystopia.

## Locked decisions

- **Title:** 전세 게임 (Jeonse Game) / English *The Deposit*.
- **Payout logic (subrogation-based):** win = clean legal payoff (the only outcome that actually reduces Tae-sung's real debt); loss = the Office pays the tenant in full and buys the claim, converting the debt to the Office on predatory terms with no legal ceiling.
- **Season architecture — Model A (per-deposit, calendar-driven):** each arc is tied to a single tenant's contract-end date, sequenced by the expiration calendar — *not* a single elimination bracket with a lump sum at the end. Chosen because jeonse debt is inherently per-contract, so per-deposit arcs are both more thematically honest and give each tenant a real arc instead of a cameo round. Multi-tenant set pieces (the Chain round, the finale, Season 2's crunch) still exist as pressure-cookers, but the spine is arc-per-deposit with the meta-conspiracy threading through.
- **Tenants are mechanically opponents, morally right.** The friction between "he must beat them" and "they deserve to win" is the engine, not a problem to resolve.
