## Raised in planning, not yet in the bible

- **Two-directional debt market (needs reconciling).** The chat landed on the idea that a *win* should let Tae-sung buy back a portion of what he already owes the Office at a discount, using pool winnings — making the tournament a debt market that runs both ways. The bible currently frames a win only as "pay the tenant directly through legal channels." Decide whether the buy-back-Office-debt-at-a-discount layer is in or out, and if in, work it into `docs/01`.
- **"Alliance episodes" disguised as fights.** One option floated: keep rounds individually zero-sum but make the shared pool *insufficient*, so some tenant-vs-landlord matches are really "beat the house's rules together" rounds in disguise. Only the tenant-vs-tenant version (the Chain round) is captured. Decide whether to build any cooperative-underneath-adversarial rounds.
- **Mid-Season-1 turn — Tae-sung quietly feeding wins.** Discussed as the moment his relationship with the tenants stops being fixed and becomes a round-to-round choice (competing on the Office's ledger while deliberately losing to specific tenants). Season 2 mentions him "steering games to protect tenants," but the earlier Season-1 inflection point isn't beat-sheeted anywhere.
- **Legal fact-check before any writers' room / publication.** The bible makes fairly specific claims about how HUG and Korean tenant-priority law (전입신고 / 확정일자 / 우선변제권 / 대항력) actually work. These are dramatized from a web search, not a legal source — verify against current statute and HUG dispute/delay mechanics before anything ships.
