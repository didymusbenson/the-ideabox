# Season Arcs

## Season 1 — "입주" (Move-In)
Tae-sung loses the money, enters the Office's system, and plays purely to save himself. Ends with the reveal that tenants are being recruited into later rounds — his first game against Kang Mi-jin closes the season on a cliffhanger choice: throw the match to protect her son's surgery fund, or protect his own remaining runway.

## Season 2 — "체납" (Default)
The syndicate widens; Da-eun starts investigating the Office itself; Tae-sung starts quietly steering games to protect tenants rather than himself, at real cost. Detective Han's outside investigation starts closing in, forcing Tae-sung to choose between confessing (ending the games, ending his freedom) or staying in long enough to make his tenants whole first. Park Sung-min's mirror arc ends in his ruin, showing what Tae-sung's path avoided.

This is also the season where multiple tenant contracts land in the same window — a genuine multi-default crisis rather than a sequence of clean one-at-a-time arcs, forcing Tae-sung to actually choose who gets paid when he can't cover everyone at once.

## Season 3 — "정산" (Settlement)
Full reveal of Director Baek's business model and its scale — hundreds of buildings, thousands of tenants, a shadow economy running parallel to the legitimate housing market. The endgame isn't "win the tournament," it's dismantling it: Tae-sung, his former tenants, Da-eun, and even Detective Han have to cooperate to expose the Office publicly, which requires Tae-sung to surrender himself to prosecution as the price of the evidence being credible. Finale closes on the tenants getting their deposits back through legitimate channels — slower, less cinematic, and the point.
