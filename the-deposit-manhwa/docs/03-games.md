# The Games

Games are designed around a rule: **every game mechanic is a metaphor for a real jeonse-system failure**, so the tournament reads as satire, not spectacle for its own sake.

| Round | Game | Mechanic | Metaphor |
|---|---|---|---|
| 1 | **역경매 (Reverse Auction)** | Contestants bid *down* how much they'll accept, sealed-bid; lowest offer "wins" but takes the worst payout ratio | The race-to-the-bottom exploitation of leveraged jeonse loans |
| 2 | **기다림 (The Wait)** | An endurance/patience trial where the money grows the longer you refuse to cash out early — but early cash-out is the only guaranteed payout | The two-year lock-in tenants are trapped in, and the temptation of predatory early-exit "buyout" scams |
| 3 | **바둑 대국 (Baduk Match)** | Straight strategy game, but board control is tied to real capital wagered per move | Long-game financial literacy vs. improvisation — Halmoni Seo's showcase episode |
| 4 | **감정평가 (The Appraisal)** | Teams must independently value a property using partial, deliberately falsified documents; biggest gap between your valuation and "the truth" loses your stake | Inflated appraisals used to justify oversized jeonse loans — the actual mechanism of real-world jeonse fraud |
| 5 | **연쇄 (The Chain)** | A multiplayer game where each contestant's payout depends on the person after them paying in — collapses if anyone defects | A literal Ponzi structure; the emotional gut-punch round where the group turns on each other |
| Finale | **정산일 (Settlement Day)** | No hidden rules, no gimmick — contestants simply choose, in front of each other, who gets paid first out of an insufficient pool | Strips away the game dressing entirely to show what "who gets their deposit back" actually means when there isn't enough money |

Games escalate from clever/procedural (Rounds 1–3) to openly cruel and psychologically weaponized (Rounds 4–6), mirroring Tae-sung's growing awareness of what the Office actually is.
